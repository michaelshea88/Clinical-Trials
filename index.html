Mining ClinicalTrials.gov

Data mining 40,000+ oncology studies on ClinicalTrials.gov

View on GitHub Download .zip Download .tar.gz

ClinicalTrials.gov is a government-funded registry of over 200,000 clinical trials. Since 2007, virtually all major studies of drugs and biologics have been required by law to be registered on the site.

The site is maintained by the National Institutes of Health and lists studies conducted in all 50 states and 192 countries. Each trial page contains a detailed description of the conditions, interventions and outcomes being studied, as well as the study design, enrollment and funding sources. For example, here is the page dedicated to Lung-MAP, a trial currently being overseen by Friends of Cancer Research ( additional information about the trial can be found at www.lung-map.org).

We searched the registry for all oncology-related trials with start dates from January 2007-present. We found over 40,000 oncology-related trials from the site and came up with a few questions that we hoped the data could shed light on. The goal is to provide a general overview of the clinical development landscape in oncology.
